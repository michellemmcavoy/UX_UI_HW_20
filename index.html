Rescue Riders: Pet Transport

Reimagining Rescue Riders’ website so that future pet owners experience intuitive navigation that builds confidence in using Rescue Riders’ transportation services.

Project Type: Conceptual design

My Role: User Research, Wireframing, Brand Design, Prototyping, Usability Testing

Duration & Tools: 3 Weeks (2023); Figjam, Figma, Adobe Suite

Overview | Background | User Research | Definition & Synthesis | Ideation | Prototyping & Testing | Outcomes | Next Steps

Overview

According to the American Pet Products Association, 34% of dogs are purchased from breeders, and only 23% of dogs are adopted from shelters. The main reason for this is fashion--most people want a specific purebred or designer dog. Buying bred pets is more popular than adoption because people have a larger selection from a breeder or a pet store than from shelters. When buying from animal shelters, buyers are typically limited to the selection in their local area. What if buyers could have a broader selection when adopting from a shelter? This is where the Rescue Riders come in; they connect buyers with their perfect-match pet, even if the animal is states away, and they transport the pet to the buyer.

The Problem

Rescue Riders' process of transporting pets across state lines is inherently complex, but their website does little to demystify the transport process or make booking information accessible for users. Not only does Rescue Riders’ website lack an intuitive way to find critical information, but also its outdated site design fails to convey a sense of trustworthiness. Both of these factors deter potential users from taking advantage of Rescue Riders' transportation services, which in turn limits their adoption range. Moreover, this denies many pets a second-chance at a forever home.

Solution

Background

Competitor Analysis

While looking at four direct competitors in the non-profit pet transport space, we wanted to understand what was successful about each non-profit websites and services, as well as what could be improved upon to make the pet transport process easier for buyers.

Among their competitors, Rescue Riders offered the largest variety of transport services, online booking, and client support. Even though all of these pet transportation services shared a similar mission they didn't all do it in a humane or ethical way. To give some perspective, the competitor Rescue Express warns new pet owners that they do not stop for bathroom breaks and to pardon the messy condition of their pet upon arrival as it is the price of life.

Our main finding was that Rescue Riders had the most robust services and compassionate approach in the pet transportation space. But, there was an opportunity to create a website structure that streamlined their booking process and UI design that enhanced their credibility.

User Research

User Interviews

In order to receive more in-depth insights on how people think about pet adoption and transportation, we conducted 5 interviews to capture those insights. We used convenience sampling to interview 5 adults who had prior experience with pet adoption.

Objectives for 1:1 Interviews
  1. Uncover which factors influence individuals' decisions to adopt a pet.
  2. Understand how individuals typically go about the process of adopting a pet.
  3. Understand if individuals have experience with or knowledge of pet transportation services when adopting pets from across the country and how do they feel about it.
Website Usability Test

To get a quick baseline measure of RR’s existing website experience, we conducted 5 usability tests. The main objectives of our usability test was to determine if users could make a donation, find adoptable animals and requirements for transport, as well as book a ride for a pet.

Despite the high success rate for most tasks, users commented that they often had to search for the right information, felt confused when navigating the site, and felt a lack of trust in the organization based on the visuals.

Persona

We summarized users' goals and frustrations into a single persona. Meet Dylan Sutton. Dylan needs to evaluate if a pet transportation service is right for her because her ideal dog which aligns with her specific training and temperament requirements is currently in another state.

Research Synthesis

After synthesizing our research into a prioritization matrix, the following emerged as the highest priority for Rescue Riders and the highest impact for users:

  1. Users need a better understanding of the transportation and booking process.
  2. Users needed a better way to sort through and filter pet transportation information. The current website uses PDFs to house the requirements for transport and schedules, which are not indexable on the web or accessible for users.
  3. Users failed to trust the service based on the outdated design and defunct navigation.
Roadmap

We prioritized turning RR’s transportation information PDFs into actual web pages to increase usability, as well as boost Search Engine Optimization to drive more traffic to the site. Further, we sought to clearly define RR’s step-by-step transport process to make it easily digestible for users to understand and follow through with booking a ride.

Ideation

Site Map

With our features prioritized, we dove into structuring the information architecture of the Rescue Riders site. We reconsidered the current sitemap by placing clarity, comprehension, and intuitive navigation as our goals. A key task was to create web pages for information that was previously linked to the website as a PDF.

Brainstorm

Also through a team brainstorming session, we came up with several ideas to increase the credibility and experience of the site. Three major key ideas emerged:

Personalize the checkout process by adding a treat or toy to a pet’s ride

Highlighting previous users' testimonials and reviews to increase the trustworthiness of the site.

Feature a ‘Rider of the Month’ which would narrate a pet’s journey from the animals’ POV, which demonstrates the care and conditions of the pet.

Prototyping & Testing

Low-Fidelity Prototype

To map out our user flows, we sketched a revamped home page, dedicated testimonials page, a new transportation information section, as well as the pet transport check out process.

Mid-Fidelity Prototype & Usability Testing

The goal of our mid-fi prototype was to confirm that our navigation improvements aligned with how users wanted to utilize our website. We primarily focused on transportation info, testimonials, and the book now process.

Mobile users had trouble finding the book now button within the hamburger, so we added an additional button to the homepage.

Both desktop and mobile users struggled to find the transportation schedule and drop-off locations. We leveraged this insight for our A/B tests.

The header logo did not always return to home, and this made navigation inconsistent. This was an easy fix, and we adjusted prototype functionality.

Branding Style

A bold color palette of orange, blue, and lime was selected to create a vibrant and playful brand image. Orange was selected as a primary color because, in color psychology, orange is meant to convey a sense of enthusiasm and adventure.This color selection plays into the idea that the pets are on their grand adventure to their forever homes. The design also leverages authentic photography to represent a realistic view into the lives of the shelter pets on the transport.

Hi-Fidelity A/B Testing

To examine how the transportation information could be more seamless for users, we developed two layouts in the high-fidelity version for the transportation locations.

Version A

Our “Version A” being a preview of upcoming dates by region. Version A provides the information at a glance.

Version B

“Version B” being a call-to-action button which users can click to see the full schedule if needed. Version B follows progressive disclosure.

After conducting 20 tests, we found that version B provided a more comprehensible and intuitive user experience. Hi-Fidelity usability testing revealed success in completing all user tasks (LIST TASKS). So, we focused our attention towards qualitative feedback regarding user impressions and attitudes towards the site.

Users felt suspicious of all testimonials being 5-star reviews, so we thought of new ways for users to read about past Rescue Riders’ experiences.

Users were eager to begin the booking process when searching for logistical information but mentioned they might not know some of the pet information at the time of booking.

The homepage had redundant step-by-step process information, but did not include any testimonials or reviews.

Next Steps

Book a Ride

We continue to have some open questions related to Rescue Rider’s operations, mission, and goals. Additionally, we would want to ensure that stakeholder feedback and priorities are understood for final prototype development and iteration.

Create a Personal Profile

We would like to consider developing a profile creation step alongside booking the transport so users can return to booking information, easily communicate with Rescue Riders, and reduce the “transactional” feeling of the current book now process.

Integrate with Rescue Partners

To make a more seamless process, we want to investigate how Rescue Riders could integrate with their rescue partners’ adoption processes by including pet profile pages, comprehensive information on rescue partners, and connecting between the websites.
